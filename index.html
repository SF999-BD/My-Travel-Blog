🌊My Saint Martin Trip: Memories of Blue Waters and White Sands

🏝️ Introduction:

For travel lovers in Bangladesh, Saint Martin's Island holds a very special place. Located about 9 km south of Teknaf in the Bay of Bengal, this tiny coral island feels like a gift of nature itself. After planning for quite a while, I finally set off with some friends to visit this dream destination last winter.

🚤 The Journey Begins

We took a bus from Dhaka to Teknaf early in the morning. From Teknaf jetty, ships and trawlers carry passengers to the island. We boarded a tourist ship, and those few hours of sailing were nothing short of magical. The endless blue waters, rolling waves, and the hazy view of the island in the distance filled the journey with excitement.

🏝️ Arrival at the Island ,

Stepping onto Saint Martin felt like stepping into another world. Clear blue waters, white sandy beaches, and the gentle sea breeze instantly refreshed us. The island is small, and there are very few vehicles—bicycles and vans are the main mode of transport, which makes the environment peaceful and quiet.

🏨 Accommodation

We stayed at a cozy beach-side guesthouse. The rooms were simple but comfortable, and the view from the balcony was breathtaking. Waking up to the sound of waves and the sight of the sun rising over the sea was an experience I will never forget.

🏖️ Exploring the Beaches

The main attraction of Saint Martin is its beaches. We spent hours walking along the shore, collecting shells, and swimming in the clear waters. The beach was not crowded, which allowed us to enjoy the tranquility and beauty of nature. The water was warm and inviting, perfect for a refreshing dip.

🌴 Nature's Beauty

The island is also home to some unique flora and fauna. We saw various types of birds, crabs, and colorful fish. The coral reefs around the island are a haven for marine life, and snorkeling here is a must-do activity. The underwater world was vibrant and full of life, a true spectacle of nature.

🍴 Tasting the Food:

No trip to Saint Martin is complete without tasting the local seafood. So, after taking some rest, we went for our lunch. There were a lots of restaurant with different local items. After roaming for a while, we went to a restaurant which was just beside the beach. As a result, we got a view of the sea with our lunch. For lunch, we enjoyed
  • Grilled Lobster
  • Fried Rupchanda Fish
  • Coconut Water



The coconuts here taste sweeter and more natural than anywhere else. The seafood is fresh, and the flavors are unforgettable. Still now, I can recall the taste of that grilled lobster and the aroma of the fried fish. My mouth waters just thinking about it!

🌅 Sunset Beauty

Watching the sunset from the beach was an unforgettable experience. The golden-red light reflected on the water made the sea look like it was covered in liquid gold. The sound of waves, the cool breeze, and the colors of the sky—it felt like time had stopped.

🌌 The Night Experience

At night, the sky was filled with stars—so many, so bright, something you can never see in the city. We sat on the beach, gazing at the starry sky, singing, and sharing stories. The nights on the island are calm, magical, and soothing to the soul.

🌊 A Visit to Chera Dwip

The next morning, we took a boat to Chera Dwip, a tiny island just off Saint Martin. It was even quieter and more pristine. The water was crystal clear, and we could see fish and corals beneath us. The raw beauty of nature here left a lasting impression on my heart.

🛍️ Things I Bought

Saint Martin is famous for it's local items which is more authentic then outside. So, we decided to buy something from there. So, we went to the local bazar for buying local items. I bought some, which I still cherish as souvenirs of this beautiful trip:
  1. Shell & Conch Crafts
  2. Coconut Shell Handicrafts
  3. Local Spices
  4. Dry Fish

💭 Final Thoughts

Saint Martin is not just a trip—it's an experience that refreshes the mind and soul. For anyone looking to escape the chaos of everyday life, this little island is a piece of paradise that everyone should visit at least once.
